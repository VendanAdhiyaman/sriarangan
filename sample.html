Taking the Stress out of Piping Engineering

Pi Engineering is a consulting company made up of experienced and highly trained piping and pipeline engineers specializing in stress analysis and finite element analysis (FEA). Our team delivers practical piping engineering solutions within Alberta and the global oil and gas sectors for facilities, equipment, and pipelines.

50+ Years of Piping Engineering Experience

Located in Calgary, Canada, Pi Engineering (Pi) has been operating since 2006, providing piping engineering expertise within Calgary, Alberta and Globally.

Our business has established itself on strong, repeat client relationships maintained by delivering quality, practical, and timely work.

Pi Engineering is a team of professionals passionate about all things piping and stress analysis. We believe in developing expertise through specialization, training, and mentorship. Weve developed a team of leading piping and pipe stress engineers and consultants.

Our Services

Piping Stress Analysis & Piping Engineering

Stress analysis is our core expertise. Pi Engineering provides a wide suite of piping engineering and stress analysis services.

Learn More

Pipeline Engineering

Our team of qualified stress engineers has extensive understanding of CSA Z662 requirements and specifications of both buried and above ground piping.

Learn More

Finite Element Analysis (FEA)

Our team has extensive experience doing FEA of piping and vessels, thermal studies, pipe supports, vessel nozzles and supports/clips, and specialty components (flanges, clamp connectors, proprietary designs).

Learn More

Vibration Measurement & Analysis

We self-perform vibration measurements. Our data recorders can track pressures, temperatures, strains, and vibrations simultaneously and continuously for weeks at a time. Measurements are recommended when vibration causes are difficult to determine from analysis and/or field observations.

Learn More

Transient Analysis & Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)

Designing piping for static loading is only a part of the picture. We rarely see field issues or failures resulting from static loading. The problems we are retained to solve are more often related to dynamic loading.
